ABOUT DR PAUL HARVIE

ORTHOPAEDIC HIP & KNEE SURGEON

Associate Professor of Orthopaedic Surgery
BEng(Hons), MBChB, MRCS, FRCS, MD, FRACS, FAOrthA

Dr Paul Harvie is a Hobart-based orthopaedic surgeon with a particular focus on hip and knee conditions, including arthritis, joint degeneration, sports injuries and orthopaedic trauma. His practice includes primary and revision joint replacement surgery, robotic-assisted knee surgery, and anterior approach hip replacement.

Associate Professor Harvie works within a collaborative surgical team to support patient care throughout the perioperative period. Surgical patients are reviewed daily by trusted medical colleagues, ensuring continuity of care and careful monitoring during recovery.

Dr Harvie performs patient specific total hip replacement using a minimally invasive direct anterior approach, incorporating assessment of spino-pelvic motion to assist with component positioning. He also performs robotic-assisted total and partial knee replacement surgery, as well as revision hip and knee replacement procedures. He offers hip resurfacing using both metal and ceramic bearing options with patient-specific instrumentation. Dr Harvie also performs radiofrequency ablation for selected patients with hip and knee arthritis or trochanteric bursitis, where appropriate.

Dr Paul Harvie consults from a purpose-designed orthopaedic suite in central Hobart, with convenient ground floor access and nearby parking.

Expertise & Background

Associate Professor Harvie is a UK-trained orthopaedic surgeon holding dual accreditation with the Royal College of Surgeons of England and the Royal Australasian College of Surgeons. Originally trained as an engineer, he completed an undergraduate engineering degree before pursuing medicine. He undertook a Medical Research Doctorate at the Botnar Research Centre, University of Oxford, prior to completing the six-year Oxford Orthopaedic Training Program. Following this, he undertook additional fellowship training in Perth (Australia), Oxford and Bristol, with a focus on complex primary and revision hip and knee replacement surgery.

In addition to his clinical practice, Associate Professor Harvie is involved in teaching and research in orthopaedics.

TREATMENTS

Anterior Hip Replacement Surgery

Dr Paul Harvie performs total hip replacement using the direct anterior approach, alongside other contemporary surgical techniques. The choice of approach is determined following careful assessment of each patient’s anatomy, diagnosis, lifestyle requirements, and surgical goals.

He incorporates detailed pre-operative planning and, patient specific instrumentation to support implant positioning and alignment. Consideration of spino-pelvic motion may also form part of surgical planning to assist with component orientation.

Where suitable, muscle-sparing techniques are utilised to facilitate early mobilisation and structured rehabilitation. The overall aim is to relieve pain, improve joint function, and support a safe return to daily activities.

Robotic-Assisted Knee Replacement Surgery

Dr Paul Harvie performs robotic-assisted total and partial knee replacement surgery as part of his management of knee arthritis and joint degeneration. Robotic technology is used to assist with detailed pre-operative planning and intra-operative guidance, supporting accurate bone preparation and implant positioning tailored to each patient’s individual anatomy.

Where appropriate, patient-specific planning is utilised to assess the natural alignment and joint characteristics of the knee prior to surgery. This information helps guide implant positioning in a way that reflects the individual structure and soft tissue balance of each patient’s joint.

Kinematic alignment principles may also be considered during knee replacement surgery. This approach seeks to restore the patient’s native joint alignment rather than applying a uniform alignment to all knees. The aim is to support balanced knee movement and functional stability following surgery.

Robotic assistance is incorporated where suitable to enhance planning accuracy and surgical execution. The overall objective is to improve knee function, reduce pain associated with arthritis, and support a structured return to daily activities.

Revision Hip & Knee Replacement Surgery

Dr Paul Harvie performs revision hip and knee replacement surgery for patients experiencing ongoing pain, implant wear, instability, loosening, or other complications following previous joint replacement procedures.

Revision surgery requires careful assessment, advanced planning, and consideration of bone quality, soft tissue integrity, and prior implant positioning. Each case is individually evaluated to determine the most appropriate surgical strategy.

Dr Harvie has undertaken fellowship training focused on complex primary and revision joint replacement surgery. His approach involves detailed pre-operative planning and structured post-operative care to support recovery and joint function.

Dr Paul Harvie will review your symptoms, medical history, and any relevant imaging. He will discuss how your hip or knee condition is affecting your daily activities and outline appropriate treatment options, including both non-surgical and surgical approaches where indicated. The aim is to ensure you have clear information to make an informed decision about your care.

If surgery is recommended, you will receive detailed written and verbal instructions regarding pre-operative preparation. This may include information about fasting, medication management, hospital admission, and planning for post-operative physiotherapy. The practice team remains available to assist with any questions leading up to your procedure.

Surgery is performed at Hobart Private Hospital or Royal Hobart Hospital using contemporary surgical techniques and careful pre-operative planning. Where appropriate, robotic-assisted technology may be utilised. Post-operative care focuses on early mobilisation, appropriate pain management, and monitored recovery in hospital. Length of stay varies depending on the procedure and individual circumstances.

Recovery involves structured rehabilitation, physiotherapy guidance, and scheduled follow-up appointments to monitor progress. Dr Harvie will review your recovery, wound healing, mobility, and overall function, adjusting your rehabilitation plan where appropriate to support steady and safe improvement.
